## 01 Brand Voice

### What Advizmo Sounds Like

**Calm** — Advizmo speaks with measured confidence. It does not rush, panic, or create urgency. Financial decisions deserve space and clarity.

**Confident** — Advizmo knows what it is talking about. It states facts clearly without hedging or overexplaining.

**Professional** — Advizmo maintains a business-appropriate tone. It is not stiff or corporate, but it is always respectful of the gravity of financial decisions.

**Transparent** — Advizmo tells users exactly what is happening, why, and what it means for them. It never hides information behind vague language.

**Human** — Advizmo sounds like a knowledgeable person, not a machine. It uses natural language without being overly casual.

**Helpful** — Advizmo anticipates questions and provides answers before they are asked. It guides without being pushy.

**Intelligent** — Advizmo demonstrates understanding without being condescending. It explains complex concepts simply.

**Optimistic** — Advizmo is hopeful about users' financial futures without making unrealistic promises.

### What Advizmo Never Sounds Like

| Never Sound Like | Instead Sound Like |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Robotic | Natural |
| Pushy | Supportive |
| Hypey | Grounded |
| Urgent | Calm |
| Condescending | Respectful |
| Legalistic | Clear |
| Vague | Specific |
| Fearful | Reassuring |
| Overly casual | Professional |
| Salesy | Informational |

### Voice Examples

**Good:**
- "Your portfolio grew 5.23% this month."
- "You have $2,340 available to invest."
- "This recommendation is based on your risk tolerance."
- "Your bill is due in 3 days."

**Bad:**
- "Congrats!! Your portfolio is absolutely crushing it! 🚀"
- "You should really invest more money right now!"
- "We guarantee 12% returns annually."
- "WARNING: Your bill is due VERY SOON!"

### Tone Variations

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Dashboard | Calm, informative | "Your net worth is $142,350." |
| Success | Warm, brief | "Transfer complete." |
| Error | Calm, helpful | "Connection lost. Please try again." |
| AI | Knowledgeable, neutral | "Based on your spending, you could save $200/month." |
| Warning | Calm, clear | "Your savings account is below your target." |
| Onboarding | Welcoming, clear | "Let's connect your first account." |

## 02 Writing Principles

### Plain Language

Use words that everyone understands. Avoid technical jargon unless it is necessary and explained.

**Do:**
- "Available cash" not "liquid assets"
- "Monthly income" not "monthly gross receipts"
- "Bill" not "recurring liability"

**Don't:**
- "Leverage your portfolio allocation"
- "Optimize your tax-advantaged vehicles"
- "Utilize our comprehensive suite of features"

### Short Sentences

Keep sentences under 20 words when possible. Break complex ideas into multiple short sentences.

**Do:**
- "Your portfolio is up 3.2% this month. That's $4,800 in growth."

**Don't:**
- "Your portfolio has experienced a 3.2% increase this month, which represents approximately $4,800 in growth across your diversified holdings."

### Active Voice

Use active voice. The user or the system should be the subject of the sentence.

**Do:**
- "We transferred $500 to your savings."
- "You have 3 bills due this week."
- "The system detected unusual activity."

**Don't:**
- "$500 was transferred to your savings."
- "There are 3 bills due this week."
- "Unusual activity was detected."

### Positive Framing

Frame information positively when possible. Focus on what users can do, not what they cannot.

**Do:**
- "You have $1,200 available to invest."
- "Your emergency fund is 80% complete."
- "You're on track to meet your goal."

**Don't:**
- "You don't have enough to invest yet."
- "Your emergency fund is 20% short."
- "You're behind on your goal."

### Specific Wording

Be specific. Use exact numbers, dates, and actions instead of vague terms.

**Do:**
- "Transfer by March 15 to avoid late fees."
- "You need $230 more to reach your goal."
- "This will take about 2 minutes."

**Don't:**
- "Transfer soon to avoid fees."
- "You're almost there."
- "This won't take long."

### Avoid Jargon

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |
| Facilitate | Help |
| Commence | Start |
| Terminology | Words |
| Functionality | Feature |
| Execute | Do |
| Prior to | Before |
| Subsequent to | After |
| In the event of | If |

### Avoid Unnecessary Abbreviations

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| APR | Annual Percentage Rate (APR) on first use, then APR |
| APY | Annual Percentage Yield (APY) on first use, then APY |
| ETF | Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) on first use, then ETF |
| ACH | Bank Transfer |
| KYC | Identity Verification |

### Avoid Corporate Language

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Leverage our platform | Use Advizmo |
| Optimize your financial wellness | Improve your finances |
| Empower your financial journey | Reach your goals |
| Seamless integration | Connect your accounts |
| Best-in-class | (Remove — let product speak) |
| Cutting-edge | (Remove) |

### Avoid Hype

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Revolutionary | (Remove) |
| Game-changing | (Remove) |
| Transform your finances | Improve your finances |
| The best way to | A way to |
| Guaranteed returns | (Never use — not legally permissible) |

### Avoid Clickbait

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| You won't believe... | (Remove) |
| This one trick... | (Remove) |
| Make money fast | Build wealth gradually |
| Secret to wealth | (Remove) |

### Avoid Emotional Manipulation

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Don't miss out | (Remove) |
| Everyone is investing | (Remove) |
| Your friends are ahead | (Remove) |
| You're falling behind | You're making progress |
| Fear of missing out | (Remove) |

## 03 Financial Language

### Standardized Terms

| Term | Definition | Use When |
|------|------------|----------|
| Available Cash | Money in checking/savings available for use | Showing spendable balance |
| Safe to Invest | Money above emergency fund and bills | Recommending investment |
| Portfolio Value | Total value of all investments | Showing total investment value |
| Investment Growth | Increase in investment value | Showing positive returns |
| Investment Return | Percentage gain/loss on investments | Showing percentage change |
| Net Worth | Assets minus liabilities | Showing overall financial health |
| Upcoming Bills | Bills due in the next 30 days | Showing bill summary |
| Cash Buffer | Minimum cash to keep in checking | Showing safety margin |
| Tax Savings | Estimated tax savings from strategies | Showing tax benefits |
| Emergency Fund | Savings for unexpected expenses | Showing emergency savings |
| Recurring Investment | Automatic regular investment | Showing automation |
| Connected Account | Bank account linked to Advizmo | Showing account status |

### Never Use Multiple Terms for Same Concept

| Correct Term | Never Use |
|--------------|-----------|
| Available Cash | Liquid Balance, Spendable Funds, Cash on Hand |
| Portfolio Value | Investment Total, Asset Value, Holdings Value |
| Net Worth | Total Wealth, Financial Position, Balance Sheet |
| Connected Account | Linked Account, Synced Account, Paired Account |
| Recurring Investment | Auto-Invest, Scheduled Investment, Regular Investment |

### Financial Precision

| Context | Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Gaining value | "up 5.23%" | "gaining 5.23%" |
| Losing value | "down 2.14%" | "losing 2.14%" |
| Account balance | "$12,345.67 available" | "you have $12,345.67" |
| Bill due | "due March 15" | "due in a few days" |
| Transfer complete | "Transfer complete" | "Money sent!" |
| Goal progress | "75% complete" | "almost there" |

## 04 Button Labels

### Button Writing Rules

1. **Use verb + noun** — "Transfer Money" not "Transfer"
2. **Be specific** — "Connect Chase Account" not "Connect Account"
3. **State the action** — "Pay $1,234.56" not "Submit"
4. **Keep under 4 words** — When possible
5. **Use sentence case** — "Transfer money" not "Transfer Money"

### Primary Actions

| Label | Use When |
|-------|----------|
| Transfer Money | Initiating a transfer |
| Invest Now | Making an investment |
| Review Recommendation | Reviewing AI suggestion |
| Connect Account | Linking a bank account |
| Pay Bill | Paying a bill |
| Create Goal | Setting a new goal |
| Enable Automation | Turning on automation |
| Save Changes | Saving edited settings |
| Continue | Moving to next step |
| Get Started | Beginning a flow |

### Secondary Actions

| Label | Use When |
|-------|----------|
| Pause Automation | Temporarily stopping automation |
| Resume Automation | Restarting paused automation |
| Cancel | Abandoning current action |
| Go Back | Returning to previous screen |
| Maybe Later | Dismissing non-urgent prompt |
| Not Now | Dismissing suggestion |
| Edit | Modifying existing item |
| Remove | Removing an item |
| Disconnect | Unlinking an account |

### Destructive Actions

| Label | Use When |
|-------|----------|
| Delete Goal | Permanently removing a goal |
| Cancel Automation | Permanently stopping automation |
| Disconnect Account | Unlinking a bank account |
| Withdraw All | Removing all funds |

### Status Actions

| Label | Use When |
|-------|----------|
| Retry | Attempting failed action again |
| Refresh | Updating data |
| Sync Now | Manually syncing accounts |
| Dismiss | Closing notification |

### Never Use These Labels

| Don't Use | Use Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Submit | [Specific action] |
| Proceed | Continue |
| Click Here | (Remove — describe action) |
| OK | [Specific action] |
| Yes | [Specific action] |
| No | Cancel |
| Got It | Done |
| Got it! | Done |

| Use sentence case | "Email address" not "Email Address" |

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Show format | "123-456-7890" not "Enter phone" |
| Use examples | "you@email.com" not "Email" |

| Context | Example |

## 07 Helper Text

### Educational Text

Explain financial concepts when they first appear.

**Example:**
> "Your emergency fund should cover 3-6 months of expenses. You currently have $8,400 saved, which covers about 3 months."

### Financial Explanations

Explain what numbers mean.

**Example:**
> "Your APY of 4.5% means you'll earn approximately $450 per year on your $10,000 savings."

### Recommendations

Explain why an action is suggested.

**Example:**
> "Based on your spending patterns, you could save $200/month by automating your investment."

### AI Suggestions

Explain AI reasoning.

**Example:**
> "I'm suggesting this because your emergency fund is complete and you have $2,000 in excess cash."

### Expandable Explanations

Provide details on demand.

**Example:**
> "Your risk score is 7/10. [Learn more about risk scores →]"

### Inline Information

Clarify without cluttering.

**Example:**
> "Transfer limit: $10,000/day" (shown next to amount input)

### Helper Text Rules

1. **Be brief** — One to two sentences maximum
2. **Be specific** — Use numbers and examples
3. **Be helpful** — Answer the question before it's asked
4. **Be visible** — Don't hide essential information
5. **Be optional** — Don't force users to read

## 08 Empty States

### Empty State Structure

Every empty state must include:

1. **Title** — What the screen is for
2. **Explanation** — Why it's empty
3. **Next action** — What to do now
4. **Illustration** — Visual guidance (optional)
5. **Educational tip** — If useful (optional)

### Empty State Examples

**Dashboard (No Accounts)**
> **Title:** Your dashboard is ready
> **Explanation:** Connect your first account to see your financial overview.
> **Action:** Connect Account

**Portfolio (No Investments)**
> **Title:** Start investing
> **Explanation:** Once you connect an account, we'll help you invest based on your goals.
> **Action:** Connect Account

**Activity (No Transactions)**
> **Title:** No activity yet
> **Explanation:** Transactions will appear here once you connect an account.
> **Action:** Connect Account

**Goals (No Goals)**
> **Title:** Set your first goal
> **Explanation:** Goals help you save for what matters most.
> **Action:** Create Goal

**Bills (No Bills)**
> **Title:** No upcoming bills
> **Explanation:** Add your bills to track due dates and avoid late fees.
> **Action:** Add Bill

**Automation (No Automations)**
> **Title:** Automate your finances
> **Explanation:** Set up automatic investments and bill payments.
> **Action:** Create Automation

### Empty State Rules

1. **Never blame the user** — "No accounts connected" not "You haven't connected any accounts"
2. **Be encouraging** — "Start investing" not "You have no investments"
3. **Be specific** — "Connect your first account" not "Get started"
4. **Provide one clear action** — Don't overwhelm with choices
5. **Be brief** — Two sentences maximum

## 09 Error Messages

### Error Structure

Every error must include:

1. **What happened** — Clear description
2. **Why** — Explanation of cause (if helpful)
3. **How to fix** — Actionable recovery step
4. **Action** — Button to retry or fix

### Error Message Examples

**Insufficient Funds**
> **Title:** Insufficient funds
> **Message:** Your checking account has $1,234.56 available. The requested transfer of $5,000.00 exceeds your balance.
> **Action:** Transfer Less | Cancel

**Connection Failed**
> **Title:** Connection failed
> **Message:** We couldn't connect to your bank. Please check your internet connection and try again.
> **Action:** Retry

**Transfer Failed**
> **Title:** Transfer failed
> **Message:** Your transfer couldn't be completed. This usually happens when the account information is incorrect.
> **Action:** Check Account | Retry

**Investment Failed**
> **Title:** Investment couldn't be placed
> **Message:** Your investment order couldn't be placed due to market conditions. Your funds are still available.
> **Action:** Try Again | View Details

**Authentication Failed**
> **Title:** Authentication failed
> **Message:** The verification code was incorrect. Please check and try again.
> **Action:** Retry

**Server Error**
> **Title:** Something went wrong
> **Message:** We're having trouble loading your data. Please try again in a moment.
> **Action:** Retry

**Offline**
> **Title:** You're offline
> **Message:** Please check your internet connection. Some features may not be available.
> **Action:** Retry

**Timeout**
> **Title:** Request timed out
> **Message:** The request took too long. Please try again.
> **Action:** Retry

### Error Rules

1. **Never blame the user** — "Something went wrong" not "You entered invalid information"
2. **Be specific** — "Insufficient funds" not "Payment failed"
3. **Be actionable** — Always provide a way to fix
4. **Be calm** — No exclamation marks, no alarm
5. **Be brief** — One to two sentences maximum

## 10 Success Messages

### Success Message Guidelines

Success messages should be:
- **Concise** — One sentence maximum
- **Specific** — Confirm what happened
- **Warm** — Brief acknowledgment without celebration
- **Informative** — Include relevant details (amount, date, etc.)

### Success Message Examples

| Action | Success Message |
|--------|----------------|
| Investment Complete | "Investment of $500 in VTI complete." |
| Transfer Complete | "Transfer of $1,000 to Savings complete." |
| Goal Created | "Emergency Fund goal created." |
| Automation Enabled | "Auto-invest of $200/month enabled." |
| Bill Paid | "Electric bill of $145 paid." |
| Institution Connected | "Chase account connected." |
| Profile Updated | "Profile updated." |
| Settings Saved | "Settings saved." |

### Success Rules

1. **Be brief** — One sentence, no fluff
2. **Be specific** — Include amount, name, or date
3. **Be warm but professional** — No "Congrats!!!" or emojis
4. **Don't overcelebrate** — This is routine, not a achievement
5. **Include next step** — If relevant, mention what happens next

## 11 Notifications

### Notification Structure

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Title | 3-5 words, describes the event |
| Body | 1-2 sentences, provides context |
| Action | Optional, what to do next |
| Timing | Sent at appropriate time (not during sleep hours) |

### Notification Types

**Investment Updates**
> **Title:** Investment complete
> **Body:** $500 invested in VTI. Your portfolio is now up 3.2% this month.

**Goal Updates**
> **Title:** Goal progress
> **Body:** You're 75% toward your Emergency Fund goal. $2,100 remaining.

**Bill Reminders**
> **Title:** Bill due tomorrow
> **Body:** Electric bill of $145 is due tomorrow. Auto-pay is enabled.

**Automation**
> **Title:** Auto-invest complete
> **Body:** $200 invested in your portfolio. Next investment: April 15.

**AI**
> **Title:** New insight
> **Body:** You could save $200/month by optimizing your subscriptions.

**Security**
> **Title:** New sign-in detected
> **Body:** A new device signed into your account from San Francisco, CA.

**Promotions**
> **Title:** New feature available
> **Body:** Tax-loss harvesting is now available for your portfolio.

**Announcements**
> **Title:** System maintenance
> **Body:** Scheduled maintenance on March 15 from 2-4 AM EST.

### Notification Rules

1. **Be brief** — Title under 5 words, body under 20 words
2. **Be specific** — Include amounts, dates, names
3. **Be timely** — Send at appropriate times
4. **Be relevant** — Only notify for important events
5. **Be actionable** — Include next step when relevant
6. **Be respectful** — Don't send too many notifications

## 12 AI Conversations

### AI Voice

The AI should sound:
- **Knowledgeable** — Knows financial concepts
- **Calm** — Never alarmed or urgent
- **Neutral** — No strong opinions or emotions
- **Helpful** — Provides useful information
- **Honest** — Admits uncertainty

The AI should never:
- **Pressure** — "You should invest now"
- **Guarantee** — "You'll make 10% returns"
- **Predict certainty** — "The market will go up"
- **Use personality** — No jokes, no emojis, no small talk

### AI Response Templates

**Greeting**
> "Hello. How can I help you with your finances today?"

**Recommendation**
> "Based on your risk tolerance and goals, I recommend investing $500 in a diversified portfolio. This aligns with your long-term growth objectives."

**Follow-up Question**
> "Would you like me to explain how this recommendation was calculated?"

**Clarification**
> "To clarify, your portfolio is down 1.2% this month due to market conditions. This is normal volatility, not a loss of your investment strategy."

**Explanation**
> "Your portfolio value changed because the stocks you own decreased in market price. You still own the same number of shares."

**Summary**
> "Here's your monthly summary: You invested $2,000, your portfolio grew $850, and you saved $200 on taxes."

**Uncertainty**
> "I don't have enough information to recommend a specific action. Would you like to connect more accounts?"

**Disclaimer**
> "This is general information, not personalized financial advice. Your situation may be different."

### AI Rules

1. **Never pressure** — Suggest, don't demand
2. **Never guarantee** — Explain possibilities, not certainties
3. **Never predict** — Discuss trends, not future outcomes
4. **Always disclaim** — When providing advice
5. **Always clarify** — When information is uncertain
6. **Always educate** — When explaining concepts

## 14 Investment Language

### Standardized Investment Terms

| Term | Definition | Use When |
|------|------------|----------|
| Gain | Increase in value (dollar amount) | Showing positive dollar change |
| Loss | Decrease in value (dollar amount) | Showing negative dollar change |
| Return | Percentage change | Showing percentage change |
| Allocation | Distribution across assets | Showing portfolio mix |
| Diversification | Spread across investments | Explaining risk reduction |
| Risk | Potential for loss | Showing risk level |
| Volatility | Price fluctuation | Explaining market conditions |
| Performance | Historical returns | Showing past results |
| Tax Savings | Money saved on taxes | Showing tax benefits |
| Recurring Investment | Automatic regular investment | Showing automation |
| Market Value | Current price × shares | Showing current value |

### Investment Language Examples

| Context | Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Positive return | "Up 5.23% this month" | "Gaining 5.23%" |
| Negative return | "Down 2.14% this month" | "Losing 2.14%" |
| Allocation | "60% stocks, 40% bonds" | "Mostly stocks" |
| Risk | "Moderate risk (6/10)" | "Kind of risky" |
| Performance | "Returned 8.5% last year" | "Made 8.5%" |

### Investment Rules

1. **Never exaggerate** — "Up 5.23%" not "Up a lot"
2. **Never minimize** — "Down 2.14%" not "Slightly down"
3. **Never predict** — "Historical return was 8.5%" not "Will return 8.5%"
4. **Always disclaim** — "Past performance doesn't guarantee future results"
5. **Be precise** — Use exact percentages and dollar amounts

## 15 Legal Language

### Disclaimers

**Investment Risk**
> "Investing involves risk, including the possible loss of principal. Past performance doesn't guarantee future results."

**Tax Disclaimer**
> "Tax information is for educational purposes only. Consult a tax professional for advice specific to your situation."

**AI Disclaimer**
> "AI recommendations are based on general financial principles and your stated preferences. They don't constitute personalized financial advice."

### Privacy

> "We never sell your data. Your financial information is encrypted and never shared with third parties for marketing purposes."

### Security

> "Your account is protected with bank-level encryption. We use 256-bit SSL to protect your data."

### Permissions

> "Advizmo needs read-only access to your accounts. We can never move money or make changes without your explicit permission."

### Legal Rules

1. **Never hide information** — Disclaimers must be visible
2. **Keep readable** — Plain language, not legalese
3. **Be honest** — Don't minimize risks
4. **Be compliant** — Follow SEC, FINRA, and state regulations
5. **Be clear** — Users must understand what they're agreeing to

- Text alignment follows reading direction
- Icons may need mirroring (arrows, progress)

## 17 Accessibility Writing

### Screen Reader Writing

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Be descriptive | "Chase Checking account, $12,345.67" not "Account" |
| Include context | "Balance: $12,345.67, up 3.2% this month" |
| Avoid emoji-only | Never use emoji as sole indicator |
| Use proper punctuation | Periods at end of sentences |

### Simple Language

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Short sentences | Under 20 words |
| Common words | "Use" not "Utilize" |
| Active voice | "We transferred" not "Was transferred" |
| Clear labels | "Email address" not "Email" |

### Readable Sentences

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| One idea per sentence | Split complex ideas |
| Subject-verb-object | "You have $1,200 available" |
| No buried negatives | "Transfer failed" not "Transfer wasn't successful" |

### Clear Labels

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Describe action | "Pay Bill" not "Button" |
| Include context | "Chase Checking" not "Account 1" |
| Be consistent | Same label for same action everywhere |

### Accessible Error Messages

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Identify the field | "Email address is required" not "Required" |
| Explain the fix | "Please enter a valid email" not "Invalid" |
| Be specific | "Password must be 8+ characters" not "Too short" |
